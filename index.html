How a Print Gets Made

Behind the lens.

Four real stages between an empty street at 2am and a numbered print on your wall.

01

Scout

Locations are walked at night, weeks ahead — checking sightlines, traffic flow, and how artificial light pools against architecture.

02

Expose

Long exposures from 8 to 90 seconds on a locked tripod, often shot across several hours to catch the right traffic and sky.

03

Develop

Each frame is graded by hand for tone and colour — no presets — to preserve the way the light actually felt that night.

04

Edition

Approved frames are released as small numbered editions, archival‑printed and signed before they ship.

The Studio

One photographer.
No daylight.

Nocturne started as a personal project in 2014 — a way to learn a city by walking it at 2am with a tripod. A decade on, it's a small studio: one photographer, one printer, and a standing rule that nothing gets shot before sunset.

Every print sold funds the next roll of film and the next sleepless night. Editions are intentionally small — when a run sells out, that frame doesn't come back.
